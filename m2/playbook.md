## Play 1 ‚Äî Internal Pursuit Kickoff | Within 48 hours after M1

### Prepare to Shape Solutions Together

Before M2, the IA team must align on the opportunity and prepare solution possibilities.

#### Objectives

- Review Opportunity Brief from M1
- Brainstorm 2-3 solution possibilities
- Identify questions for M2 collaboration
- Prepare M2 deck for client discussion
- Confirm M2 team and attendees

#### Activities

**1. Schedule Pursuit Kickoff Meeting (60 min)**

Required attendees:
- Business Lead (meeting owner)
- Engagement Manager (delivery lead)
- Relevant SME(s) (practice area expert)
- Finance (if complex pricing needed)

**2. Kickoff Agenda:**

**Review Opportunity Brief (15 min)**
- BANT recap (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline)
- Client's top 3 priorities
- Success criteria from M1
- Non-negotiables

**Brainstorm Solution Possibilities (30 min)**
- What are 2-3 possible approaches?
- What are the trade-offs between approaches?
- What questions do we still need to answer?
- What expertise/resources will we need?
- What are the potential risks?

**Prepare M2 Deck (15 min)**
- Assign deck creation to Business Lead
- Identify what to present (possibilities, not THE solution)
- Plan for collaborative exploration, not pitch

**3. Required Outputs:**
- M2 Deck with solution possibilities
- Question list for client collaboration
- Resource Requirements Form (RRF) submitted if needed
- M2 attendees confirmed (IA side)

#### RACI Matrix

| Activity | Business Lead | EM | SME | Ops |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Schedule Kickoff | A/R | I | I | - |
| Review Opp Brief | R | R | R | - |
| Solution Brainstorm | R | R | A | - |
| M2 Deck Creation | A/R | C | C | I |
| RRF Submission | R | A | C | R |

#### ‚úì Completion Checklist

- [ ] Kickoff meeting completed
- [ ] 2-3 solution approaches identified
- [ ] M2 deck created (10-15 slides)
- [ ] Questions for client documented
- [ ] RRF submitted (if resources needed)
- [ ] M2 attendees confirmed (BL, EM, SME)
- [ ] M2 materials reviewed by team

Ensure team alignment and materials readiness.

#### Objectives

- Finalize M2 deck with solution possibilities
- Brief all IA attendees on approach
- Update Pipedrive/CRM

#### Activities

**4. Request Client Materials (if needed)**

- Any relevant documentation to review
- Access to systems (if applicable for demos)

## Play 3 ‚Äî M2 Meeting Execution | 90 minutes

### Part 1: Recap & Frame (0-10 min)

Reconnect with client and set collaborative tone.

#### Objectives

- Thank them for M1
- Demonstrate we listened (recap priorities)
- Frame today as collaboration, not pitch
- Get buy-in for approach

#### Script & Activities

**Opening (2 min)**

Script:
> "Thanks for making time today. Before we dive in, I want to recap what we heard in M1 and set expectations for today."

**Recap (5 min)**

Script:
> "Here's what stood out from our conversation:
> - You're trying to achieve [X based on M1]
> - Your top priorities are [Y, Z based on M1]
> - Success looks like [outcomes they described]
>
> Did we capture that accurately? Anything to add or adjust?"

**Frame Today (3 min)**

Script:
> "Today is about exploration and collaboration. We've developed some solution possibilities based on what you shared, but we need your input to shape the right approach.
>
> This isn't a pitch‚Äîthink of it as a working session where we design this together. Sound good?"

#### Critical Success Indicator

Are they nodding and engaged? If yes, proceed.
If they seem confused or hesitant, pause and clarify.

#### RACI Matrix

| Activity | Business Lead | EM | SME |
|---|---|---|---|
| Opening & Framing | A/R | - | - |
| Recap Priorities | A/R | C | - |

---

### Part 2: Present Solution Possibilities (10-30 min)

Share thinking, invite reactions, open dialogue.

#### Objectives

- Present 2-3 solution approaches (high-level)
- Pique interest and invite questions
- Set up for collaborative refinement
- Avoid "selling" or being prescriptive

#### Presentation Structure

**1. The Challenge (3 min)**

Cover:
- Problem statement as we understand it
- Why it matters to their business
- Cost of inaction

Keep it tight. They already know the problem.

**2. Our Point of View (5 min)**

Share:
- How we think about this type of challenge
- What we've seen work (and not work) elsewhere
- Critical success factors

This is where you establish thought leadership without pitching.

**3. Solution Possibilities (10 min)**

Present 2-3 approaches:

**Approach A: [Name it descriptively]**
- Overview (2-3 bullets)
- Key components
- Benefits and trade-offs

**Approach B: [Name it descriptively]**
- Overview (2-3 bullets)
- Key components
- Benefits and trade-offs

**Approach C: [Optional]**
- Overview (2-3 bullets)
- Key components
- Benefits and trade-offs

**Comparison slide:**
- Side-by-side of trade-offs
- "We're not recommending one yet‚Äîwe need your input"

**4. Questions We Need Answered (2 min)**

Share:
- Open questions for collaboration
- Areas where we need their input
- Unknowns we'll solve together

#### Presentation Tips

- **Pause frequently** ‚Äî Invite reactions after each approach
- **Encourage questions** ‚Äî "What questions does this raise?"
- **Stay flexible** ‚Äî Be ready to adapt based on their reactions
- **Don't oversell** ‚Äî This is exploration, not closing

#### Critical Success Indicator

**Are they asking questions and reacting?**

If yes ‚Üí They're engaged, good sign
If no ‚Üí You're talking too much; pause and ask: "What are your initial thoughts?"

#### RACI Matrix

| Activity | Business Lead | EM | SME |
|---|---|---|---|
| Present Challenge | A/R | C | C |
| Present POV | R | C | R |
| Present Solutions | R | R | A |
| Facilitate Q&A | A/R | R | R |

### Part 3: Collaborate on Solution (30-80 min)

This is the heart of M2‚Äîco-create the approach together.

#### Objectives

- Identify which approach resonates
- Understand buying committee and decision process
- Confirm final top 3 priorities
- Learn from previous vendor experiences
- Define success metrics together
- Surface and address concerns

#### Collaboration Framework

Use these question sequences to guide discussion.

**ON THE APPROACHES (10 min)**

Questions:
- "Which of these resonates most with you? Why?"
- "What would you change or adapt?"
- "What are we missing?"
- "What concerns you about each approach?"

Listen for:
- Clear preference or combination of approaches
- Specific requirements we missed
- Political considerations ("my boss would want...")
- Budget implications they're revealing

**ON DECISION-MAKING (10 min)**

Questions:
- "Who else needs to be involved in shaping this?" (buying committee)
- "Who's the ultimate decision maker?" (KDM)
- "What's your internal process from here?"
- "Who will champion this internally?"

Listen for:
- Names and titles of buying committee
- Approval chain and timing
- Politics (who might resist, who's influential)
- Budget approval process

**Pro Tip:** Draw the org chart as they talk. Visual confirmation helps.

**ON PRIORITIES - FINAL VALIDATION (10 min)**

Questions:
- "Of everything we've discussed, what are the 3 things that matter MOST?" (rank them)
- "What are your absolute must-haves?" (non-negotiables)
- "Where do you have flexibility?"
- "What trade-offs are you willing to make?"

Listen for:
- Changes from M1 (priorities evolve with solution visibility)
- Hard requirements vs. nice-to-haves
- What they'd sacrifice if budget constrained

**ON PREVIOUS EXPERIENCE (5 min)**

Questions:
- "You mentioned you've worked with [vendor] before..."
- "What did you like about how they worked?"
- "What would you want us to do differently?"
- "What did you wish they had done?"

Listen for:
- Specific complaints (how to differentiate)
- What they valued (emphasize in proposal)
- Red flags to avoid

**ON SUCCESS (10 min)**

Questions:
- "How will you measure success internally?"
- "What does 'great' look like vs. 'good enough'?"
- "What would make this a career-defining win for you?"
- "How will you report progress to leadership?"

Listen for:
- Specific KPIs and metrics
- Personal motivations (career impact)
- Reporting requirements (influence proposal structure)

#### Listening & Note-Taking Protocol

**Business Lead:** Lead questions, manage time
**EM:** Take detailed notes on scope, deliverables, timeline
**SME:** Listen for technical requirements, ask clarifying questions

**What to capture:**
- Buying committee (names, titles, roles)
- Final top 3 priorities (ranked)
- Non-negotiables
- Success metrics (specific)
- Concerns raised
- Previous vendor lessons
- Any "Aha!" moments or strong reactions

#### RACI Matrix

| Activity | Business Lead | EM | SME |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lead Questions | A/R | C | C |
| Capture Notes | R | A/R | R |
| Technical Clarification | C | C | A/R |
| Scope Refinement | R | A | R |

### Part 4: Get Permission for M3 (80-90 min)

Soft close to earn permission to write proposal.

#### Objectives

- Confirm shaped approach
- Get permission to formalize in proposal
- Schedule M3 for proposal review
- Set expectations for proposal delivery

#### The Soft Close Script

> "This has been a powerful meeting. I love the interaction and based on everything we've discussed‚Äî[recap their goals and priorities from THIS meeting]‚ÄîI'm excited about putting this on paper in a formal proposal and SOW.
>
> Here's what I'd like to propose as next steps:
> 1. We'll take what we shaped today and formalize it in a proposal
> 2. If we have any questions as we're writing it, may I follow up with you?
> 3. Can we schedule M3 in the next week to review the proposal together?
>
> Does [specific date/time] work for M3?"

**Why This Works:**
- Recaps what THEY said (not what we want)
- Asks permission (soft close, not assumptive)
- Sets specific next steps
- Proposes date/time (makes it real)

#### If They Say Yes

**Confirm:**
1. M3 date/time locked
2. Who will attend M3 (buying committee members?)
3. Who we can contact with questions during proposal writing
4. Any additional information we need
5. Confirm: "We'll send the proposal 24-48 hours before M3 so you have time to review"

#### If They Hesitate

**Option A: "I need to check with [decision maker]"**

Response:
> "Of course. Should we include [decision maker] in M3? Or would it help to have a quick call with them before we write the proposal?"

**Option B: "I'm still not sure about [X]"**

Response:
> "Let's address that now. What additional information would help?"

*Address concern, then try soft close again.*

**Option C: "This feels like a lot / too expensive"**

Response:
> "I hear you. Would it help to phase this differently? We could start with [Phase 1] to prove value, then expand?"

*Explore options, document, confirm M3 to review alternatives.*

#### RACI Matrix

| Activity | Business Lead | EM | SME |
|---|---|---|---|
| Soft Close | A/R | C | - |
| M3 Scheduling | A/R | I | - |
| Address Concerns | A/R | R | R |

#### ‚úì Completion Checklist

- [ ] Soft close delivered
- [ ] M3 scheduled (date/time confirmed)
- [ ] M3 attendees identified (client side)
- [ ] Permission granted to write proposal
- [ ] Contact for questions identified
- [ ] Expectations set (proposal 24-48 hrs before M3)

## Play 5 ‚Äî Strategy-to-Win Meeting | 90-120 minutes

### Internal Proposal Planning

This is THE critical internal meeting to write a winning proposal.

#### Objectives

- Identify win themes for THIS client
- Structure the proposal narrative
- Develop SOW with scope and deliverables
- Create LOE and pricing
- Assign proposal writing responsibilities

#### Meeting Structure

**1. Win Themes Identification (20 min)**

Questions:
- What are our unique differentiators for THIS client?
- Why should they choose us over alternatives?
- What proof points do we have? (case studies, references)
- How do we address their specific concerns from M2?

Output: 3-5 win themes to emphasize in proposal

**2. Proposal Structure (20 min)**

Define:
- Executive summary approach (hook + value prop)
- Solution narrative flow (how we tell the story)
- Key messages per section
- Visual elements (diagrams, frameworks)

Output: Proposal outline with section owners

**3. SOW Development (30 min)**

Define:
- Scope (what's in, what's out‚Äîbe specific)
- Deliverables (concrete, measurable)
- Timeline and milestones
- Resource allocation (who does what)
- Assumptions and dependencies
- Success criteria and acceptance criteria

Output: SOW outline ready to formalize

**4. LOE & Pricing (30 min)**

Create:
- Level of Effort (hours by role)
- Resource costs (blended rates)
- Pricing strategy (fixed fee, T&M, hybrid?)
- Discount considerations (if strategic fit)
- Payment terms (aligned with milestones?)

Output: Pricing model and LOE breakdown

**5. Risk Assessment (10 min)**

Identify:
- What could go wrong?
- Mitigation strategies
- Out-of-scope clarifications (prevent scope creep)

Output: Risk mitigation section for proposal

#### Required Outputs

- **Proposal/SOW** assignment (who writes what sections)
- **LOE** (hours and cost breakdown)
- **Pricing** (final number with Finance approval if needed)
- **Resource assignments** in Everhour (proposed project)
- **M3 presentation plan** (how we'll review proposal)
- **Target delivery date** (24-48 hrs before M3)

#### RACI Matrix

| Activity | Business Lead | EM | SME | Finance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Win Themes | R | R | R | - |
| Proposal Structure | A/R | R | C | - |
| SOW Development | R | A/R | R | - |
| LOE Creation | C | A/R | R | - |
| Pricing | R | R | C | A |

## Common M2 Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

### Mistake #1: Pitching, Not Collaborating

**Problem:** Coming with THE solution, not possibilities

**Fix:** Present 2-3 approaches, ask which resonates, shape together

---

### Mistake #2: Not Bringing the Team

**Problem:** BL goes solo, EM and SME don't attend

**Fix:** EM and SMEs MUST attend M2. Non-negotiable.

---

### Mistake #3: Talking Too Much (Again)

**Problem:** Presenting for 60 minutes, collaborating for 10

**Fix:** Present for 20 min max, collaborate for 50+ min

---

### Mistake #4: Not Identifying Buying Committee

**Problem:** Assuming one person decides

**Fix:** Always ask "Who else needs to be involved in this decision?"

---

### Mistake #5: Skipping "What Matters Most"

**Problem:** Not reconfirming priorities from M1

**Fix:** Explicitly ask "Of everything, what are the top 3 things that matter MOST?"

---

### Mistake #6: No Clear Next Steps

**Problem:** Good meeting, but no M3 scheduled

**Fix:** ALWAYS secure M3 before leaving M2

---

### Mistake #7: Not Writing Proposal Immediately

**Problem:** Waiting days/weeks to write proposal, momentum dies

**Fix:** Strategy-to-Win within 24 hours, proposal written within 5 days

---

### Mistake #8: Skipping Strategy-to-Win

**Problem:** Winging the proposal, inconsistent messaging

**Fix:** Mandatory 90-min S2W meeting with full team

## M2 Success Metrics

### You've had a successful M2 if:

‚úÖ **Client is actively engaged** and contributing ideas  
‚úÖ **Buying committee is identified** (names, roles)  
‚úÖ **Top 3 priorities confirmed** and ranked  
‚úÖ **Previous vendor lessons** understood  
‚úÖ **Solution approach co-created** (not just presented)  
‚úÖ **M3 is scheduled** with decision makers  
‚úÖ **Permission granted** to write the proposal  
‚úÖ **Internal team knows** exactly what to propose  
‚úÖ **Win themes identified** for this specific client  

### Red Flags (Proceed with Caution)

‚ùå Low engagement in M2 (not asking questions)  
‚ùå Can't identify decision maker or buying committee  
‚ùå Major scope or budget concerns unresolved  
‚ùå Conflicting priorities among stakeholders  
‚ùå No urgency or timeline shifting  
‚ùå "Let us think about it" without M3 commitment
