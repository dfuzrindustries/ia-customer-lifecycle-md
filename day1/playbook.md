## Phase 00 â€” Pre-Workshop Preparation (1 Week Before)

### Play 0.1: Pre-Workshop Discovery

**Objective:** Gather context to maximize workshop productivity

#### Activities

1. **Intake Call (30 min)**
   - Review company overview, org chart, tech stack
   - Identify known pain points and strategic priorities
   - Confirm workshop attendees and roles

2. **Materials Request**
   - **Strategic Documents:** Strategic plan, Vision/Mission/Purpose statement, current OKRs or quarterly goals, recent board presentation
   - **Organizational Context:** Org chart showing key functions and reporting structure, brief company overview or pitch deck
   - **Current State:** List of KPIs tracked, any existing business model or strategy canvas documentation
   - **Technology Landscape:** High-level tech stack overview (CRM, HRIS, finance systems, product analytics)
   - **Optional:** Process documentation, technology architecture diagrams, prior transformation initiative results

3. **Prep Package Delivery**
   - Send pre-read materials (Maturity Model overview, workshop agenda)
   - Share pre-workshop questionnaire (optional)

#### RACI

| Role | Assignment | Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| BS | R, A | Leads all pre-workshop activities; owns workshop success |
| AI, TS | C | Review materials and flag key insights |
| CEO | I | Receives prep package |

#### Outputs

- Pre-workshop brief (2-3 pages) summarizing company context
- Hypothesis list of potential high-value workflows

## Agenda Item 01 â€” Welcome & Framework Introduction

**Time:** 9:00 AM - 9:30 AM (30 minutes)

### Play 1.1: Opening & Relationship Building

**Objective:** Establish trust, set tone, align on outcomes

#### Script Framework

1. **Welcome (5 min)**
   - EL introduces IA team, asks each person to introduce themselves
   - CEO shares 2-minute context: why now, what success looks like

2. **Workshop Purpose (10 min)**
   - EL presents: "This is not discovery. This is diagnostic and prioritization."
   - Review agenda and timing
   - Set ground rules: candor, participation, focus on outcomes not activities

3. **Execution Gap Framework (10 min)**
   - EL presents: Technology promise vs. business reality
   - Key stat: 70% of digital investments underdeliver
   - Frame: "We're here to identify 1-3 workflows that will either save you money or make you money"

4. **Expected Outcomes (5 min)**
   - EL previews: SOW for 3 POC workflows + supporting documentation
   - Confirm decision timeline and next steps

#### RACI

| Role | Assignment | Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| BS | R, A | Facilitates entire session; owns agenda and flow |
| CEO | C | Provides strategic context |
| AI, TS | I | Listen and take notes on strategic priorities |

#### Tools/Materials

- Slide deck (10 slides max)
- Execution Gap framework visual
- Workshop agenda handout

#### Success Criteria

- CEO and attendees understand workshop purpose
- Ground rules established
- Energy level is high and collaborative

## Agenda Item 02 â€” Business Canvas - Strategy & AI Readiness

**Time:** 9:30 AM - 11:00 AM (90 minutes)

### Play 2.1: Business Canvas Discovery

**Objective:** Map strategic business model and identify 8-12 critical workflows

#### Facilitation Approach

**Part 1: Strategic Foundation (30 min)**

Map the Business Canvas components:

1. **Vision, Mission, Purpose (VMP)**
   - "What is your vision for the company 3-5 years out?"
   - "What is your core missionâ€”the problem you exist to solve?"
   - "What is your purpose beyond profit?"

2. **Brand & Culture**
   - "How would you describe your brand identity?"
   - "What are 3-5 words that describe your culture?"
   - "What cultural strengths/weaknesses impact transformation?"

3. **Strategic Objectives & OKRs**
   - "What are your top 3-5 strategic priorities this year?"
   - "What are your current OKRs or quarterly goals?"
   - "Where are you ahead/behind on these objectives?"

**Part 2: Value Stream Mapping (55 min)**

Map workflows across the 4 value engines:

**Growth Engine Questions:**
- "Walk me through your customer acquisition process from first touch to closed deal"
- "Where do deals get stuck or slow down?"
- "What manual work consumes the most time in sales/marketing?"
- "How do you prioritize leads or opportunities?"

**Retention Engine Questions:**
- "How do you identify customers at risk of churn?"
- "Walk me through onboarding a new customer"
- "How do customer success teams know when to intervene?"
- "What renewal or expansion processes are most manual?"

**Efficiency Engine Questions:**
- "What operational processes are most error-prone or slow?"
- "Where do you have the most rework or quality issues?"
- "What reporting or analysis takes the longest to produce?"
- "What manual approvals create bottlenecks?"

**Innovation Engine Questions:**
- "How do you decide what to build next?"
- "How do you measure product adoption or feature usage?"
- "What feedback loops take longest to close?"

**Part 3: Workflow Prioritization (15 min)**

1. **Whiteboard capture** (20 min): List all workflows mentioned
2. **Clustering** (10 min): Group similar workflows
3. **Prioritization** (15 min): Which workflows have highest pain/impact?
4. **Selection** (10 min): Narrow to 8-12 workflows for deeper assessment

#### RACI

| Role | Assignment | Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| BS | R, A | Leads facilitation; ensures comprehensive coverage |
| CEO, FL | C | Describe workflows and pain points |
| AI | C | Asks technical feasibility questions; identifies automation potential |
| TS | C | Assesses user experience and adoption challenges |

#### Success Criteria

- Business Canvas captured: VMP documented, culture themes identified, current OKRs listed
- 8-12 workflows identified with clear owners
- Workflows span multiple value streams
- Pain points and impact are articulated
- Clear line of sight from OKRs to workflow opportunities

### Play 2.2: AI Readiness Assessment

**Objective:** Assess organizational readiness to implement AI-powered workflows

#### Assessment Framework

Score each workflow (1-5) across 5 dimensions:

**1. Data Availability** (AI leads)
- 1 = No data or data in silos
- 3 = Data exists but requires cleanup
- 5 = Clean, accessible, real-time data

**Questions:** "What data exists for this workflow? Where is it stored? How accessible? How clean/complete? Can we get a sample dataset?"

**2. Technical Infrastructure** (AI leads)
- 1 = Legacy systems, no APIs
- 3 = Modern systems, some integration capability
- 5 = API-first architecture, cloud-native

**Questions:** "What systems are involved? Do they have APIs? What's the integration complexity? Any security/compliance constraints?"

**3. Process Maturity** (BS leads)
- 1 = Ad hoc, tribal knowledge
- 3 = Documented but inconsistent
- 5 = Standardized, optimized, measured

**Questions:** "Is this workflow documented? How consistent is it across teams? Are there SOPs? Who owns it?"

**4. Change Capacity** (TS leads)
- 1 = Change-resistant culture
- 3 = Moderate openness, some resistance
- 5 = Innovation-hungry, early adopter culture

**Questions:** "How receptive are users to new tools? Past adoption successes/failures? Any change fatigue?"

**5. Business Impact Potential** (BS leads)
- 1 = Low frequency, low value
- 3 = Moderate frequency and value
- 5 = High frequency, high strategic value

**Questions:** "How often is this workflow executed? How many people? What's the cost of errors or delays? Strategic importance?"

#### Scoring Process

1. **Individual Scoring** (20 min): Each IA team member scores workflows independently
2. **Calibration** (15 min): Team discusses scores, resolves discrepancies
3. **Composite Score** (10 min): Average scores across 5 dimensions
4. **Ranking** (10 min): Sort workflows by composite readiness score

#### RACI

| Role | Assignment | Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| BS | A | Owns overall assessment process |
| AI | R | Leads Data & Tech Infrastructure scoring |
| BS | R | Leads Process Maturity & Business Impact scoring |
| TS | R | Leads Change Capacity scoring |
| CEO, FL | C | Provide data for scoring |

#### Success Criteria

- All 8-12 workflows scored across 5 dimensions
- Team consensus on scores
- Workflows ranked by readiness
- Top 5-7 workflows identified for deeper dive

## Agenda Item 03 â€” Operational Canvas & Transformation Mapping

**Time:** 11:15 AM - 12:45 PM (90 minutes)

### Play 3.1: Maturity Assessment

**Objective:** Establish current-state baseline across 9 functions

#### Assessment Process

Using the Intelligent Agency Maturity Model (Levels 1-5), assess maturity for each function:

**9 Functions:**
1. CEO / Strategy
2. Product
3. Marketing
4. Sales
5. Accounts / CSM
6. Delivery / Operations
7. HR / People
8. IT
9. Finance

**5 Maturity Levels:**
- **Level 1:** Ad Hoc (reactive, no KPIs, manual)
- **Level 2:** Functional Foundation (basic processes, some measurement)
- **Level 3:** Cross-Functional Alignment (integrated, data-driven)
- **Level 4:** AI-Augmented Operations (automation, predictive insights)
- **Level 5:** AI-Native Enterprise (autonomous agents, real-time intelligence)

#### Facilitation Approach

1. **Overview** (10 min): Present Maturity Model framework
2. **Functional Assessment** (60 min): 
   - Spend 6-7 min per function
   - Ask CEO/FL to describe current state
   - BS scores on 1-5 scale with rationale
   - Capture key gaps and constraints
3. **Heat Map Creation** (20 min):
   - Plot all 9 functions on heat map
   - Identify patterns (e.g., strong product, weak ops)
   - Highlight critical gaps for transformation

#### Key Questions Per Function

**CEO / Strategy:**
- "How often do you review KPIs? What cadence?"
- "Do you have OKRs? Are they measured and tracked?"
- "How data-driven are strategic decisions?"

**Product:**
- "How do you measure product-market fit?"
- "What's your discovery and roadmap process?"
- "How do you track feature adoption?"

**Marketing:**
- "Do you track ROI on marketing spend?"
- "How mature is your attribution model?"
- "What's your MQL to SQL conversion process?"

**Sales:**
- "How accurate is your forecast?"
- "Do you have a defined sales process?"
- "How do you qualify and prioritize pipeline?"

*(Continue for all 9 functions)*

#### RACI

| Role | Assignment | Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| BS | R, A | Leads assessment; scores functions |
| CEO, FL | C | Describe current state for each function |
| AI, TS | C | Provide observations and technical/adoption context |

#### Success Criteria

- All 9 functions scored (1-5)
- Heat map visualized
- Key gaps identified
- Average maturity score calculated

---

### Play 3.2: Gap Analysis & Prioritization

**Objective:** Identify highest-impact transformation opportunities

#### Activities

1. **Gap Identification** (30 min)
   - For each low-scoring function, identify 1-3 critical gaps
   - Connect gaps to strategic objectives
   - Estimate impact of closing each gap

2. **Opportunity Mapping** (25 min)
   - Map each workflow (from 2.1) to maturity gaps
   - Identify which workflows close which gaps
   - Assess alignment to strategic objectives

3. **Impact Ã— Feasibility Matrix** (20 min)
   - Plot workflows on 2x2 matrix
   - High Impact + High Feasibility = Priority 1
   - Identify "quick wins" and "strategic bets"

#### Prioritization Framework

Score each workflow:

**Impact Score (1-5):**
- Revenue impact
- Cost reduction
- Strategic alignment
- User volume affected

**Feasibility Score (1-5):**
- Data readiness
- Technical complexity
- Change management difficulty
- Timeline to value

**Priority Calculation:** Impact Ã— Feasibility = Priority Score

#### Success Criteria

- All workflows plotted on Impact Ã— Feasibility matrix
- Top 5-7 workflows clearly identified
- Rationale documented for each priority score
- Team consensus on prioritization

## Agenda Item 04 â€” Workflow Prioritization & POC Scoping

**Time:** 1:30 PM - 3:00 PM (90 minutes)

### Play 4.1: Collaborative Prioritization Workshop

**Objective:** Select final 3 POC workflows with executive buy-in

#### Facilitation Approach

1. **Present Findings** (15 min)
   - Review Business Canvas
   - Show Maturity Assessment heat map
   - Present top 5-7 prioritized workflows

2. **Group Discussion** (45 min)
   - For each top workflow, discuss:
     - Business case (impact, ROI potential)
     - Technical feasibility
     - Resource requirements
     - Risk factors
   - Identify dependencies or conflicts
   - Adjust priorities based on executive input

3. **Final Selection** (20 min)
   - Narrow to 3 POC workflows
   - Confirm executive sponsor support
   - Validate resource availability

4. **Scope Refinement** (10 min)
   - Define MVP scope for each POC
   - Set success metrics
   - Identify required data and systems

#### Decision Criteria

- **Strategic Alignment:** Does it support top 3 OKRs?
- **Impact Potential:** ROI > 3x within 12 months?
- **Feasibility:** Can we prove value in 30 days?
- **Sponsorship:** Does it have executive champion?
- **Portfolio Balance:** Mix of quick wins and strategic bets?

#### RACI

| Role | Assignment | Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| BS | R, A | Facilitates prioritization; documents decisions |
| CEO | A | Final decision authority on POC selection |
| FL | C | Provide functional expertise and constraints |
| AI, TS | C | Validate feasibility and adoption potential |

#### Success Criteria

- 3 POC workflows selected
- Executive sponsor identified for each
- Success metrics defined
- Scope boundaries clarified

---

### Play 4.2: POC Success Criteria Definition

**Objective:** Define measurable success criteria for each POC

#### Activities

For each POC workflow, define:

1. **Hypothesis Statement**
   - "We believe that [AI solution] will [measurable impact] for [user group] by [mechanism]"

2. **Success Metrics (Quantitative)**
   - Time savings: "X hours saved per week"
   - Cost reduction: "$Y saved annually"
   - Quality improvement: "Z% error reduction"
   - User adoption: "W users actively using within 30 days"

3. **Acceptance Criteria (Qualitative)**
   - User satisfaction score â‰¥ 7/10
   - Positive feedback from at least 70% of test users
   - No critical bugs or blockers

4. **Go/No-Go Thresholds**
   - **GO:** 2 of 3 success metrics achieved + positive user feedback
   - **NO-GO:** <1 success metric achieved or negative user feedback
   - **ITERATE:** Mixed results requiring refinement

#### Template

| POC Workflow | Hypothesis | Success Metrics | Acceptance Criteria | Go/No-Go Threshold |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Workflow 1] | [Statement] | [3-5 metrics] | [Criteria] | [Thresholds] |
| [Workflow 2] | [Statement] | [3-5 metrics] | [Criteria] | [Thresholds] |
| [Workflow 3] | [Statement] | [3-5 metrics] | [Criteria] | [Thresholds] |

#### Success Criteria

- Hypothesis defined for each POC
- 3-5 quantitative metrics per POC
- Acceptance criteria documented
- Go/no-go thresholds agreed

## Agenda Item 05 â€” SOW Review & Next Steps

**Time:** 3:00 PM - 3:30 PM (30 minutes)

### Play 5.1: Draft SOW Walkthrough

**Objective:** Present draft Statement of Work for POC phase

#### SOW Components

1. **Executive Summary**
   - 3 selected POC workflows
   - Expected business impact
   - Timeline and investment

2. **POC Scope (per workflow)**
   - Workflow description
   - Success metrics
   - Technical approach
   - Data requirements
   - User testing plan

3. **Timeline**
   - 30-day POC schedule
   - Key milestones
   - Decision points

4. **Investment**
   - POC phase: $75,000
   - Resource requirements (IA + Client)
   - Assumptions and dependencies

5. **Success Framework**
   - Go/No-Go decision criteria
   - Pilot phase proposal (if GO)

#### Presentation Approach

1. **Present SOW** (15 min): Walk through each component
2. **Q&A** (10 min): Address questions and concerns
3. **Adjustments** (5 min): Capture any requested changes

#### Success Criteria

- SOW presented clearly
- Executive understands scope, timeline, investment
- Questions addressed
- Commitment to review SOW within 48 hours

### Play 5.2: Data Access & Stakeholder Alignment

**Objective:** Confirm data access and stakeholder availability for POC

#### Activities

1. **Data Access Requirements** (10 min)
   - Review data needed for each POC
   - Identify data owners and approval process
   - Confirm security and compliance requirements
   - Set timeline for data access (target: Day 2 of POC)

2. **Stakeholder Confirmation** (10 min)
   - Identify product owners for each POC
   - Confirm functional user availability for testing (5-10 per POC)
   - Validate IT/technical liaison availability
   - Schedule POC kickoff meeting

3. **Communication Plan** (5 min)
   - Establish Slack channel or communication platform
   - Set cadence for status updates
   - Define escalation path

#### Success Criteria

- Data access owners identified
- Stakeholders confirmed and available
- Communication channels established
- POC kickoff scheduled
