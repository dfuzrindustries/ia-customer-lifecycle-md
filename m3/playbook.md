## Play 1 ‚Äî Proposal Writing | Between M2 and M3

### Write a Winning Proposal

Time available: 5 days maximum between M2 and M3

#### Objectives

- Write comprehensive proposal incorporating M2 collaboration
- Develop detailed SOW with scope and deliverables
- Create LOE and finalize pricing
- Get internal approvals (GM, Finance, Legal)
- Deliver to client 24-48 hours before M3

#### Activities

**1. Write Proposal Document (Business Lead + EM)**

Typical length: 12-18 pages

**Proposal Structure:**

**Section 1: Executive Summary** (1-2 pages)
- Their challenge (as they described it)
- Our solution (high-level)
- Expected outcomes (specific, measurable)
- Investment summary

**Section 2: Understanding** (2-3 pages)
- Current state
- Challenges and impacts
- Why now? (urgency trigger)
- Success criteria (from M2)

**Section 3: Proposed Solution** (4-6 pages)
- Overall approach (shaped in M2)
- Key phases/components
- Deliverables per phase
- Timeline with milestones
- How it addresses their top 3 priorities

**Section 4: Why Intelligent Agency** (2-3 pages)
- Win themes (specific to THIS client)
- Relevant experience and case studies
- Team qualifications
- Differentiators vs. alternatives

**Section 5: Success & Measurement** (1 page)
- KPIs and metrics (from M2)
- How we'll track progress
- Definition of success
- Reporting cadence

**Section 6: Investment** (1-2 pages)
- Pricing and structure
- Payment terms
- What's included
- Optional add-ons (if applicable)

**Section 7: Next Steps** (1 page)
- How we move forward
- Timeline to signature and kickoff
- Roles and responsibilities

**2. Write Statement of Work (SOW)** (EM + BL)

Critical elements:
- **Scope of work** (what's in, what's OUT‚Äîbe explicit)
- **Deliverables** (specific, measurable, with acceptance criteria)
- **Timeline** (phases, milestones, durations)
- **Roles & responsibilities** (IA and client)
- **Assumptions** (what must be true for this to work)
- **Dependencies** (what client must provide)
- **Out of scope** (prevent scope creep)
- **Payment terms** (tied to milestones)
- **Change management** (how we handle changes)

**3. Create Level of Effort (LOE)** (EM)

Breakdown by:
- Role (AI Architect, Engineer, PM, etc.)
- Hours per role
- Blended rate or individual rates
- Total cost
- Contingency (if applicable)

Format: Excel spreadsheet (only share if client requests)

**4. Get Internal Approvals**

Required approvals:
- **GM approval** if:
  - Gross margin < 30%
  - Deal > $1M
  - Strategic discount requested
  
- **Finance approval** if:
  - Complex pricing structure
  - Payment terms non-standard
  - Revenue recognition implications
  
- **Legal approval** if:
  - Custom MSA needed
  - Unusual terms or IP considerations
  - Compliance requirements (SOC2, HIPAA, etc.)

**5. Deliver to Client (24-48 hours before M3)**

Email template:
```
Subject: [Client Name] - Proposal for [Project Name]

Hi [Name],

I'm excited to share the proposal we discussed in our M2 meeting.

As we shaped together, this approach focuses on [priority 1], [priority 2], and [priority 3]. We've structured it to deliver [main outcome] by [key date].

Attached you'll find:
- Full proposal (understanding, approach, team, investment)
- Statement of Work (scope, deliverables, timeline)
[- Level of Effort (if requested)]

Please take time to review before our M3 meeting on [date/time]. Come with questions‚Äîwe want to make sure this is exactly right.

Looking forward to discussing on [day].

[Your name]
```

Attach:
- Proposal (PDF)
- SOW (PDF or Word for comments)
- LOE (Excel, if requested)

## Play 2 ‚Äî Pre-M3 Internal Prep | Day Before M3

### Prepare to Close

Internal prep is essential for successful M3.

#### Objectives

- Anticipate questions and objections
- Rehearse key messages and win themes
- Decide on deal flexibility (what we can/can't give)
- Align team on M3 roles

#### Activities

**1. Hold Internal M3 Prep Meeting (30 min)**

When: Day before M3
Who: Business Lead, EM, SME(s) who will attend M3

**Agenda:**

**Anticipate Questions (15 min)**
- What will they push back on?
- Where might they have concerns?
- Price? Scope? Timeline? Team?
- What clarifications might they need?

Create a "Question Bank" with prepared responses.

**Rehearse Key Messages (10 min)**
- Win themes (why us?)
- Value proposition (why this approach?)
- Risk mitigation (why now?)

Practice the soft close script.

**Decision on Deal Flexibility (5 min)**
- Where can we be flexible? (scope, timeline, price)
- Where are we NOT flexible?
- What requires GM approval?
- Who has authority to negotiate on the call?

**2. Define M3 Roles**

**Business Lead:**
- Opens meeting, frames discussion
- Leads proposal walkthrough
- Addresses business questions
- Delivers soft close

**Engagement Manager:**
- Answers scope/timeline questions
- Addresses operational concerns
- Takes notes on changes

**SME:**
- Addresses technical questions
- Confirms feasibility of changes

**3. Update Pipedrive/CRM**

- Status: "M3 Scheduled"
- Probability: 75%
- Next action: M3 date/time
- Note: Proposal delivered [date]

|---|---|---|---|

## Play 3 ‚Äî M3 Meeting Execution | 60 minutes

### Part 1: Frame the Meeting (0-5 min)

Set collaborative tone and confirm they've reviewed.

#### Objectives

- Thank them for reviewing
- Set expectations (collaborative refinement)
- Confirm they've read it
- Surface big-picture reactions

#### Opening Script

> "Thanks for taking the time to review the proposal. Before we dive in, I want to frame today's conversation. This isn't a 'take it or leave it' meeting‚Äîwe want to make sure we've got this exactly right.
>
> Our goal is to address your questions, make any necessary adjustments, and if it feels right, agree on next steps to move forward together."

#### Confirm They've Reviewed

Ask:
- "Have you had a chance to review the proposal?"
- "What questions came up as you read it?"
- "Before we go through it section by section, are there any big-picture reactions?"

**If they HAVEN'T reviewed it:**

Response:
> "No problem. Would it make more sense to reschedule so you have time to review? Or would you like us to walk through it now and we can follow up with questions afterward?"

*Rescheduling is often better‚Äîhard to close if they haven't read it.*

### Part 2: Walk Through Proposal (5-30 min)

Review key sections, highlight win themes, invite questions.

#### Objectives

- Recap what we proposed
- Emphasize how it addresses their priorities
- Highlight win themes (why IA?)
- Surface initial questions

#### Walkthrough Structure

**Executive Summary & Understanding (3 min)**

Ask:
- "Did we capture your challenge accurately?"
- "Is this the right framing?"
- "Any adjustments needed?"

**Proposed Approach (8 min)**

Cover:
- Recap the solution we shaped in M2
- Walk through key phases/components
- Highlight how it addresses their top 3 priorities

Ask:
- "Does this approach still feel right?"
- "Any concerns or questions so far?"

**Why Intelligent Agency (5 min)**

Emphasize:
- Win themes (our differentiators for THEM)
- Relevant experience and proof points
- Team qualifications

Ask:
- "What resonates with you?"
- "What would you want to emphasize differently?"

**Timeline & Deliverables (5 min)**

Cover:
- Key milestones and phases
- Deliverables at each phase
- Critical path items

Ask:
- "Does this timing work with your constraints?"
- "Any concerns about the schedule?"

**Investment & Terms (4 min)**

Cover:
- Pricing and structure
- Payment schedule
- What's included

Ask:
- "Are these terms clear?"
- "Any questions about the investment?"

#### Facilitation Tips

- **Pause frequently** ‚Äî Invite questions after each section
- **Listen more than talk** ‚Äî Let them surface concerns
- **Don't defer** ‚Äî Address questions immediately
- **Take notes** ‚Äî EM captures all change requests

### Part 3: Address Questions & Concerns (30-50 min)

Surface and resolve all objections.

#### Objectives

- Hear all concerns and questions
- Address each one thoroughly
- Make necessary adjustments
- Remove all barriers to "yes"

#### Common Question Categories & Response Frameworks

**ON SCOPE**

**Question:** "This seems like a lot‚Äîcan we phase it?"

**Response Framework:**
1. Acknowledge: "I hear you. Let's look at how we could phase this."
2. Explain rationale: "We structured it this way because [dependencies/value]."
3. Explore alternatives: "We could start with Phase 1 [describe], prove value, then expand."
4. Confirm impact: "That would change timeline to [X] and price to [Y]. Does that work better?"

**Question:** "What if we remove [X]?"

**Response Framework:**
1. Understand: "Help me understand‚Äîwhat's driving that?"
2. Explain impact: "Removing [X] would affect [outcome/timeline/other deliverables]."
3. Offer alternatives: "Could we instead [simplify/defer/replace]?"
4. Document: "Let me capture this adjustment and we'll update the SOW."

**ON PRICING**

**Question:** "This is more than we budgeted."

**Response Framework:**
1. Empathize: "I appreciate your candor. Let's work through this."
2. Reframe value: "When we look at the ROI‚Äî[specific outcomes and value]‚Äîthe payback period is [X months]."
3. Explore options:
   - "Could we phase it to spread the investment?"
   - "What if we structured it as outcome-based pricing?" (ECIF model)
   - "We could start with a smaller scope to prove value first."
4. Know your floor: "Let me check with my team on what's possible within your budget."

**Question:** "Can you discount?"

**Response Framework:**
1. Don't cave immediately: "Help me understand what would make this work for you."
2. Get something in return: "If we adjusted the price, could we [get referral/case study/longer term/etc.]?"
3. Offer alternatives:
   - Value-adds instead of discount (training, ongoing support)
   - Payment terms flexibility
   - Phasing to reduce initial outlay
4. Escalate if needed: "Let me discuss with my GM and get back to you by EOD."

**Discount Authority:**
- BL can approve: Up to 10% discount
- GM approval needed: 10-20% discount
- C-level approval needed: >20% discount

**ON TIMELINE**

**Question:** "We need it faster."

**Response Framework:**
1. Understand driver: "What's driving the accelerated timeline?"
2. Explain constraints: "The current timeline accounts for [dependencies/testing/etc.]."
3. Explore options: "We could add resources to compress by [X weeks], but that increases cost by [Y]."
4. Be honest about risks: "If we compress further, the risk is [quality/adoption/etc.]. Is that acceptable?"

**Question:** "Can we start later?"

**Response Framework:**
1. Understand: "What's changing on your end?"
2. Check resource impact: "Let me confirm our team availability if we push to [new date]."
3. Adjust timeline: "We can adjust. New timeline would be [start date] to [end date]."
4. Confirm: "Does that work better?"

**ON TEAM**

**Question:** "Who exactly will be working on this?"

**Response Framework:**
1. Confirm team: "Your core team will be [names and titles]."
2. Share qualifications: "[Name] has [X years] experience in [relevant domain]."
3. Offer introduction: "Would you like to meet them before we start?"

**Question:** "Can we have [specific person]?"

**Response Framework:**
1. If yes: "Yes, [name] is allocated to this project."
2. If no: "Let me check [name]'s availability and get back to you by [date]."
3. If unavailable: "[Name] isn't available, but [alternative] has similar experience in [domain]. Would you like to meet them?"

#### Sales Training: Tactics to Get to YES

Remember: "What do we need to get them to say YES?"

**Option 1: Outcome-Based Pricing**
- "What if you only pay for results, not just effort?"
- Align incentives with their success

**Option 2: Phased Approach**
- "Let's start with Phase 1, prove value, then continue"
- Reduces initial commitment and risk

**Option 3: ECIF (Earn Client's Investment First)**
- "We'll deliver value before you pay full price"
- Risk reversal‚Äîwe take on the risk

**Option 4: Value-Adds**
- Include extras that cost us little but add value to them
- Training, documentation, ongoing support, tool licenses

**Option 5: Payment Terms Flexibility**
- Spread payments over time
- Align with their fiscal calendar
- Net 45 or Net 60 instead of Net 30

**Option 6: Start Now, PO Later**
- "We can begin with verbal commitment while your PO processes"
- Shows confidence and urgency

**Option 7: Investment in Relationship**
- Travel to their office for kickoff
- Bring executives to demonstrate commitment
- Provide reference calls with similar clients

**Give to Get Philosophy:**
- If you give discount, get case study or referral
- If you add scope, get faster payment or longer engagement
- Always trade, never just give away value

### Part 4: Soft Close & Next Steps (50-60 min)

Get verbal commitment and establish path to contract.

#### Objectives

- Secure verbal "yes"
- Understand approval process
- Confirm contracting timeline
- Set kickoff date

#### The Soft Close Script

> "Based on our discussion today, and the adjustments we've agreed to make [recap any changes], does this feel like the right path forward?"

**Pause. Let them respond.**

#### If They Say YES

**Immediate Actions:**

1. **Confirm Next Steps**

Say:
> "Great! Here's what happens next:
> 1. We'll send an updated SOW reflecting today's discussion by [date]
> 2. What's your internal approval process from here?
> 3. Who needs to sign the MSA and SOW on your end?
> 4. What's the timeline for signatures?
> 5. Once signed, we can kick off on [target date]"

2. **Document Verbal Commitment**

Say:
> "I'll send a follow-up email today confirming what we agreed and next steps. Sound good?"

3. **Set Expectations**

Confirm:
- Who needs to approve internally
- Legal review needed? (how long)
- PO process and timing
- Target signature date
- Target kickoff date

#### If They Hesitate

**"I need to discuss with [decision maker]"**

Response:
> "Of course. Would it help to include [decision maker] in a follow-up call? Or is there additional information you need to bring to them?"

*Offer to help facilitate, provide materials for internal advocacy.*

**"I'm still concerned about [X]"**

Response:
> "Let's make sure we address that now. What would resolve that concern?"

*Address it, adjust if needed, then soft close again.*

**"This feels like a lot / too expensive"**

Response:
> "I hear you. Let's look at alternatives:
> - What if we phased it starting with [Phase 1]?
> - What if we structured it as outcome-based pricing?
> - What budget range would work for you?"

*Explore options, document, confirm follow-up.*

**"Let me think about it"**

Response:
> "Absolutely. To help you think through it, what specific questions or concerns should we address?"

*Surface real objections, address them, set follow-up timeline.*

## Play 5 ‚Äî Contracting to Close | 1-10 Days Typically

### Navigate Approval and Contracting

The time between verbal and signed contract can make or break deals.

#### Objectives

- Support client through internal approval
- Facilitate legal review
- Maintain momentum
- Close the deal

#### Activities

**1. Stay Engaged (Without Being Annoying)**

- Check in every 2-3 days
- Offer to answer questions
- Help facilitate internal advocacy
- Provide additional materials if helpful

**Pro Tip:** Use value, not pressure
- "How can I help move this forward?"
- NOT: "When are you signing?"

**2. Support Their Internal Process**

**If they need to present internally:**
- Offer executive summary
- Provide talking points
- Share ROI model
- Make yourself available for questions

**If legal is reviewing:**
- Respond quickly to legal questions
- Be flexible on reasonable requests
- Escalate to IA legal if needed
- Track changes and negotiate collaboratively

**If procurement is involved:**
- Provide any additional documentation needed
- Be responsive to vendor forms/questionnaires
- Work with Ops to complete compliance docs

**3. Maintain Momentum**

**Red Flags (Deal Stalling):**
- No response to multiple follow-ups
- "We're still discussing internally" for >2 weeks
- Champion becomes hard to reach
- Legal review dragging with no progress

**How to Unstick:**
- Escalate to decision maker
- Offer executive-to-executive call
- Provide deadline with reasoning (e.g., resource availability)
- Consider walking away if no progress

**4. Sales Training Tactic: "Start Work with Verbal"**

If appropriate:
> "While your PO is processing, we could begin planning and kickoff prep under our verbal agreement. Would that be helpful?"

Shows confidence and urgency, gets work started.

## Common M3 Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

### Mistake #1: Sending Proposal Day of M3

**Problem:** No time for client to review

**Fix:** ALWAYS send 24-48 hours before M3. Non-negotiable.

---

### Mistake #2: Being Defensive

**Problem:** Treating questions as attacks

**Fix:** Stay collaborative. Questions = engagement. Welcome them.

---

### Mistake #3: Not Addressing Price Concerns

**Problem:** Avoiding the money conversation

**Fix:** Talk about value and outcomes. Offer options. Be creative.

---

### Mistake #4: Winging It

**Problem:** No prep for questions and objections

**Fix:** Internal M3 prep meeting is mandatory. Anticipate and prepare.

---

### Mistake #5: Not Getting Verbal Commitment

**Problem:** Good meeting, but no decision

**Fix:** ALWAYS deliver the soft close. Ask for the decision.

---

### Mistake #6: Poor Follow-Up

**Problem:** Meeting ends, no email, momentum dies

**Fix:** Follow-up email within 2 hours. Every time.

---

### Mistake #7: Not Involving Legal Early

**Problem:** Legal review delays contract by weeks

**Fix:** Get IA legal involved early if custom MSA or complex terms.

---

### Mistake #8: Celebrating Too Early

**Problem:** Verbal ‚â† Signed. Deals fall apart.

**Fix:** Stay engaged through contracting. Don't assume it's done.

---

### Mistake #9: Poor Sales-to-Delivery Handoff

**Problem:** EM not prepped, client expectations misaligned

**Fix:** Formal handoff meeting. EM fully briefed before kickoff.

## Proposal Writing Best Practices

### Win Themes to Emphasize

**1. Relevant Experience**
- Similar clients, similar challenges
- Proof points and case studies
- Testimonials and references

**2. Unique Approach**
- What makes our solution different
- Why this approach vs. alternatives
- Proprietary methods or frameworks

**3. Team Qualifications**
- Specific people, specific skills
- Years of experience in their domain
- Credentials and certifications

**4. Understanding Their Business**
- We "get" their challenge (prove it)
- We understand their constraints
- We speak their language

**5. Risk Mitigation**
- How we'll ensure success
- What could go wrong and how we'll handle it
- Track record of delivery
