# Intelligent Agency Customer Lifecycle Service Blueprint

**Mapping workflows, touchpoints, and handoffs across the complete customer journey**

---

## Service Blueprint Overview

This service blueprint maps the entire Intelligent Agency customer lifecycle from initial sales contact through long-term partnership. It shows:

- **Customer Actions** - What the client experiences and does
- **Frontstage Actions** - Client-facing activities by IA team
- **Backstage Actions** - Internal IA activities invisible to client
- **Support Processes** - Systems, tools, and infrastructure
- **Physical Evidence** - Tangible artifacts at each stage

## Key Handoffs in the Customer Lifecycle

### Handoff 1: M1 → M2 (Sales Qualification to Opportunity Shaping)

**Trigger:** BANT complete, M2 scheduled

**From:** Business Lead (solo or with light support)  
**To:** Business Lead + Engagement Manager + SME(s)

**Critical Documents:**
- Opportunity Brief (complete BANT)
- M2 date/time confirmed
- Buying committee identified

**Systems:**
- Pipedrive: Lead → Opportunity (25% → 50%)
- Schedule internal pursuit kickoff (within 48 hours)

**Failure Modes:**
- Incomplete BANT → can't shape solution effectively
- Wrong attendees in M2 → miss key stakeholders
- No EM involvement → poor solution scoping

---

### Handoff 2: M2 → M3 (Opportunity Shaping to Proposal)

**Trigger:** Solution shaped collaboratively, permission to propose

**From:** Business Lead + EM + SME(s)  
**To:** Same team (for proposal writing)

**Critical Documents:**
- Updated Opportunity Brief (refined approach, buying committee)
- M3 date/time confirmed
- Win themes identified

**Systems:**
- Pipedrive: Opportunity 50% → 75%
- Schedule strategy-to-win meeting (within 24 hours)
- Everhour: Create proposed project
- Financial Forecast: Add opportunity

**Critical Activities:**
- Strategy-to-win meeting (90-120 min)
- Write proposal/SOW
- Create LOE and pricing
- Get internal approvals (GM if <30% margin, Legal if custom MSA)

**Failure Modes:**
- Skipping strategy-to-win → weak proposal
- Missing win themes → generic pitch
- No internal approval → delays at contract
- Proposal delivered day-of M3 → no time to review

---

### Handoff 3: M3 → Contract Signing (Proposal to Signed Deal)

**Trigger:** Verbal commitment received

**From:** Business Lead + EM + SME(s)  
**To:** Business Lead (lead), Legal, Operations, Finance

**Critical Documents:**
- Verbal agreement (documented in email)
- Updated SOW (if changes from M3)
- MSA + SOW for signature
- PO# (from client)

**Systems:**
- Pipedrive: 75% → 90% (Verbal) → 100% (Signed)
- Financial Forecast: 1 → 0 (Verbal) → B (Booked)
- Win-wire sent to team
- AR Folder: Store contracts and PO

**Critical Activities:**
- Follow client's approval process
- Legal review (both sides)
- Procurement/vendor forms
- Get signed MSA + SOW
- Receive PO#

**Failure Modes:**
- Losing momentum after verbal
- Slow legal review
- Missing procurement requirements
- Not staying engaged during approval

---

### Handoff 4: Signed Contract → Day 1 Workshop (Sales to Delivery)

**Trigger:** Signed MSA, SOW, PO received

**From:** Business Lead  
**To:** Day 1 Workshop Facilitator + Team

**Critical Documents:**
- Signed SOW (defines scope, timeline, team)
- All sales discovery notes (Opportunity Brief, proposal)
- PO# and payment terms

**Systems:**
- Operations: Set up README, invoicing
- Everhour: Convert proposed project → active
- Schedule kickoff (internal and client)
- Notify EM/Project Lead

**Critical Activities:**
- Internal kickoff (team introduction, project overview)
- Client kickoff (within 1 week of signing)
- Workshop prep (review discovery, design activities)
- Confirm workshop date, attendees, logistics

**Failure Modes:**
- Poor sales-to-delivery handoff → team doesn't know context
- Missing Opportunity Brief notes → lose insights
- Delayed kickoff → momentum loss
- Workshop facilitator not briefed on sales promises

---

### Handoff 5: Day 1 → POC (Discovery to Validation)

**Trigger:** Day 1 Workshop complete, POC recommendations delivered

**From:** Day 1 Facilitator + Team  
**To:** POC Lead + AI Architect + Data Engineer + PM + QA

**Critical Documents:**
- Day 1 Workshop findings
- Prioritized POC recommendations (1-3 workflows)
- Success criteria for each POC
- POC SOW (signed)

**Systems:**
- Everhour: Set up POC project
- Allocate POC team resources
- Development environment setup

**Critical Activities:**
- POC kickoff (Day 1 of POC)
- Technical discovery and requirements
- Environment and data access setup
- Sprint planning

**Failure Modes:**
- Vague success criteria → can't measure results
- Data access delays → blocks development
- Scope creep → POC becomes pilot
- Wrong users for testing → invalid results

---

### Handoff 6: POC → Pilot (Validation to Production)

**Trigger:** POC complete, go/no-go decision made, Pilot SOW signed

**From:** POC Lead + Team  
**To:** Pilot Lead + Deployment Team + Client Stakeholders

**Critical Documents:**
- POC assessment report (DFVR scores)
- User validation results
- Go/No-Go recommendation
- Pilot SOW (for approved POCs)

**Systems:**
- Everhour: POC project → Pilot project
- Production environment provisioning
- Pilot team resource allocation

**Critical Activities:**
- Pilot kickoff (review POC learnings)
- Production environment setup
- User training plan
- Support infrastructure setup

**Failure Modes:**
- Forcing "no-go" POCs to pilot → guaranteed failure
- Not learning from POC → repeat mistakes
- Insufficient production infrastructure → unstable pilot
- No user training plan → low adoption

---

### Handoff 7: Pilot → Program (Production to Transformation)

**Trigger:** Pilot complete, ROI demonstrated, Year 1 Program SOW signed

**From:** Pilot Lead + Team  
**To:** Program Lead + Transformation Team + AI CoE

**Critical Documents:**
- Pilot completion report (ROI, adoption, playbooks)
- Year 1 transformation roadmap
- Program SOW (Year 1, 4 cycles)
- Operational playbooks

**Systems:**
- Everhour: Pilot → Program (year-long)
- Program team allocation
- Maturity assessment baseline

**Critical Activities:**
- Program kickoff (Cycle 1 planning)
- AI Center of Excellence formation
- Governance model establishment
- Cycle 1-4 planning

**Failure Modes:**
- Unclear Year 1 roadmap → no direction
- No CoE → can't sustain
- Pilot learnings not captured → lose knowledge
- Insufficient resources for 4 cycles

---

### Handoff 8: Program → Partnership (Transformation to Co-Investment)

**Trigger:** Program complete (Year 1), Level 5 maturity achieved, Partnership model agreed

**From:** Program Lead + Transformation Team  
**To:** Partnership Executive + Client Executive + Legal (both sides)

**Critical Documents:**
- Year 1 completion report (maturity assessment)
- Partnership agreement (co-investment terms)
- Joint innovation roadmap
- Legal entity structure (if spin-out/JV)

**Systems:**
- Partnership portal
- Revenue tracking
- IP management
- Joint roadmap planning

**Critical Activities:**
- Partnership structure definition
- Co-investment model agreement
- Innovation pipeline establishment
- Joint governance setup

**Failure Modes:**
- Unclear partnership value → no buy-in
- Legal complexity → delays
- No innovation pipeline → partnership stalls
- Misaligned incentives → conflict

---

## Critical Success Factors at Each Handoff

### Quality Inputs
- Complete documentation from prior phase
- Clear success criteria
- Lessons learned captured
- Context preserved

### Clean Handoff Process
- Formal handoff meeting
- Document review and Q&A
- System updates complete
- Team introductions

### Clear Ownership
- RACI for new phase defined
- Accountability established
- Escalation paths clear
- Support available

### Continuity
- Client relationship maintained
- Momentum preserved
- Expectations managed
- Value demonstrated
